Mission Statement

Since there exists even the faintest spark, we have a responsibility to turn it into an anti-cancer drug that can be shared by all.

We are not merely developing a drug; we are exploring a new model — a virtuous cycle between science and capital, and the meeting point of scientific progress with humanity’s shared destiny.

Science-Driven + Blockchain-Transparent

Project 667 applies rigorous methods to validate the anti-cancer potential of naturally derived compound formulas. One track fast-tracks supplements to generate cash flow; the other advances drug registration and clinical trials. All funding and buyback actions are recorded on-chain for transparency and verifiability.

“Project 667” Dual-Track Strategy

We validate efficacy, safety, and mechanisms through standardized experiments, building auditable data assets that evolve into evidence acceptable to clinicians and regulators.

Both tracks proceed in parallel: real market feedback and cash flow sustain continuous research, while we steadily pursue drug registration and clinical trials along mainstream medical pathways.

Why dual-track?
The supplement path can quickly bring cash flow and real user validation. But to enter mainstream medicine and truly benefit patients worldwide, drug registration and clinical trials are essential. Running both tracks in parallel secures short-term sustainability and long-term breakthroughs.

Dual-Track Exploration

Supplement Path · Cash Flow

  • Ingredient analysis and standardization for rapid market entry
  • Launch health products to validate direction through real users and real revenue
  • Build cash flow to feed back into research
  • Allocate 10%–15% of profits to token buybacks and burns
  • User feedback accelerates and grounds scientific progress

Pharmaceutical Path · Clinical & Registration

  • Focus on mechanism of action and clinical studies, progressing toward drug registration
  • Build dual trust in science and efficacy across global mainstream markets
  • Mechanism research → Clinical application → Phased clinical trials
  • Post-launch: ongoing buybacks and transparent mechanisms
