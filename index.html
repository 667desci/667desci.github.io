Blue Ocean · Future Outlook

Market Opportunity

The global anti-cancer drug market exceeds $100 billion annually and continues to expand.

More than 60% of approved cancer drugs are derived from natural sources, yet the vast majority are based on single molecules. In contrast, compound formulas and multi-target approaches remain almost entirely unexplored, leaving a massive unmet need and opportunity.

Historically, natural compound research has been limited by complex compositions and difficulties in standardization, which made clinical translation rare. Today, however, the landscape is changing: multi-omics integration, systems pharmacology, and artificial intelligence are breaking down these barriers. Combined with blockchain-enabled transparent research, natural compound formulas can now be studied, validated, and industrialized in ways never before possible.

This convergence of market demand and technological iteration opens the door for 667desci to capture a unique opportunity.

Dual-Track Strategy

Technological progress is unlocking a field once beyond reach, yet the path to pharmaceutical registration remains complex and uncertain. To balance opportunity with stability and ensure that innovation translates into real‑world impact, we adopt a dual‑track development strategy: one track accelerates early cash flow and real‑world validation through health supplements; the other steadily advances toward pharmaceutical registration and clinical trials, building the scientific and regulatory trust required for mainstream medicine. This approach maintains momentum while paving the way for long‑term breakthroughs.

Dual-Track Exploration

Supplement Path · Cash Flow

  • Ingredient analysis and standardization for rapid market entry
  • Launch health products to validate direction through real users and real revenue
  • Build cash flow to feed back into research
  • Allocate 10%–15% of profits to token buybacks and burns
  • Market validation driving research forward: user feedback accelerates and grounds scientific progress

Pharmaceutical Path · Clinical Trials and Registration

  • Focus on mechanism of action and clinical studies, progressing toward drug registration
  • Build dual trust in science and efficacy across global mainstream markets
  • Mechanism research → Clinical application → Phased clinical trials
  • Post-launch: ongoing buybacks and transparent mechanisms
